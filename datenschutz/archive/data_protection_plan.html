Data Protection Plan

Date: 12.8.2024

1. Purpose

This Data Protection Plan ("Plan") is established to ensure compliance with Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 27 April 2016 on the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data and on the free movement of such data, and repealing Directive 95/46/EC (General Data Protection Regulation - GDPR). This Plan outlines the measures and procedures implemented by Annurschule to protect the rights and freedoms of data subjects and ensure the lawful processing of personal data.

2. Scope

This Plan applies to all processing of personal data undertaken by AnnurSchule, including that of our employees, clients, suppliers, and other stakeholders. It covers personal data processed by automated means and in structured manual filing systems. This Plan is applicable to all departments, employees, and third-party contractors who process personal data on behalf of Annurschule.

3. Data Protection Principles

Annurschule adheres to the principles set forth in Article 5 of the GDPR:

  1. Lawfulness, fairness, and transparency: Personal data shall be processed lawfully, fairly, and in a transparent manner in relation to the data subject.
  2. Purpose limitation: Personal data shall be collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes and not further processed in a manner that is incompatible with those purposes.
  3. Data minimization: Personal data shall be adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary in relation to the purposes for which they are processed.
  4. Storage limitation: Personal data shall be kept in a form which permits identification of data subjects for no longer than is necessary for the purposes for which the personal data are processed.
  5. Integrity and confidentiality: Personal data shall be processed in a manner that ensures appropriate security of the personal data, including protection against unauthorized or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction, or damage, using appropriate technical or organizational measures.

4. Data Subject Rights

Annurschule respects and facilitates the rights of data subjects as outlined in Chapter 3 of the GDPR:

  1. Right to be informed (Articles 13 and 14): We provide clear and concise privacy notices at the point of data collection.
  2. Right of access (Article 15): Data subjects can request access to their personal data and receive a copy of the information we hold.
  3. Right to rectification (Article 16): Data subjects can request correction of inaccurate personal data or completion of incomplete data.
  4. Right to erasure (Article 17): Data subjects can request the deletion of their personal data under certain circumstances.
  5. Right to restrict processing (Article 18): Data subjects can request the restriction of processing of their personal data under certain conditions.
  6. Right to data portability (Article 20): Data subjects can request to receive their personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format under certain conditions.
  7. Right to object (Article 21): Data subjects can object to the processing of their personal data under certain circumstances.
  8. Rights related to automated decision-making and profiling (Article 22): Data subjects have the right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing, including profiling, which produces legal effects or similarly significantly affects them.

Procedures for handling data subject requests are detailed in https://schule.annur.de/datenschutz/.

5. Measures for Protecting Data

In accordance with Article 32 of the GDPR, Annurschule implements appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk:

  1. Technical Measures:
    • Encryption: All personal data at rest is ziped and encrypted using industry-standard encryption protocols (e.g. AES-256).
    • Regular software updates: All systems and software are updated regularly with the latest security patches.
    • Access controls: Role-based access control (RBAC) is implemented to ensure users have access only to the data necessary for their role.
    • Regular backups: Automated daily backups with secure storage and regular testing of restore procedures.
    • Organizational Measures:
    • Staff training: Annual data protection training for all employees and contractors.
    • Data Protection Officer: Appointed in accordance with Article 37 of the GDPR to oversee compliance.
    • Policies and procedures: Comprehensive set of data protection policies and procedures implemented and regularly reviewed.
    • Regular audits: Internal audits conducted bi-annually to ensure compliance.

6. Data Breach Response Plan

In the event of a personal data breach, Annurschule will follow this response plan in accordance with Articles 33 and 34 of the GDPR:

  1. Containment and Recovery:
    • The Data Protection Officer (DPO) shall be notified immediately upon detection of a potential breach.
    • The DPO will assemble a response team to identify the breach source and implement immediate containment measures.
    • The response team will initiate data recovery procedures where possible.
  2. Assessment and Analysis:
    • The DPO will lead an assessment of the breach's nature, scope, and potential impact on data subjects.
    • The assessment will determine if the breach is likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons.
  3. Notification:
    • If the breach is likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, the DPO will notify the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, in accordance with Article 33 of the GDPR.
    • If the breach is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, the DPO will ensure that affected data subjects are notified without undue delay, in accordance with Article 34 of the GDPR.
    • All breaches, regardless of severity, will be documented in the Annurschule's data breach register.
  4. Evaluation and Response:
    • Following containment and notification, a thorough review of the incident will be conducted.
    • The DPO will prepare a report detailing the cause of the breach and recommendations for preventing future occurrences.
    • The Annurschule will implement any necessary changes to policies, procedures, or security measures based on the findings of the review.

7. Review and Updates

This Data Protection Plan shall be reviewed annually by the Data Protection Officer and senior management. Updates will be made as necessary to ensure ongoing compliance with the GDPR and other relevant data protection regulations. Any significant changes to this Plan will be communicated to all employees and relevant stakeholders.
